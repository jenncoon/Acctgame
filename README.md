# Boardwalk Books LLC: Accounting Cycle Game
### Crestwood Heights

An interactive, browser-based accounting cycle game for introductory
financial accounting students. Players take the role of accountant for
Boardwalk Books LLC, a real estate company investing across the fictional
city of Crestwood Heights. Designed to bring the hands-on experience of
playing Monopoly-style games in the classroom to asynchronous online courses.

## Learning Objectives

Students who complete this game will be able to:

- Record journal entries for common business transactions including
  property purchases, rent received, and rent paid
- Complete all seven phases of the accounting cycle through closing entries
- Apply either US GAAP or IFRS terminology depending on course setting
- Reflect on the relationship between debits and credits through
  a structured written prompt

## How to Play

**[Play the game here](https://jenncoon.github.io/Acctgame/)**

No installation required. Runs in any desktop or tablet browser.
Not optimized for mobile.

## Instructor Notes

- Students play against a computer opponent over two rounds, recording
  every transaction, then complete the full accounting cycle
- Supports two closing entry methods: direct to Retained Earnings or
  through Income Summary
- Supports US GAAP and IFRS terminology via in-game toggle
- A downloadable reflection card is generated at completion for
  instructor submission
- Available as a SCORM file for LMS import; can be set to assign a grade,
  complete/incomplete, or used as a page
- Designed for use in Financial Accounting (ACCT 2301) or equivalent
  introductory course
- An accessible format option is included in the instructor guide
- A second version covering bad debt and the allowance method, interest
  accrual, and straight-line depreciation is currently in development
